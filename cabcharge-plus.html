I started off by talking to key stakeholders within the business to better understand their challenges and vision.

 

Key learnings were:

• Cabcharge has over 40,000 clients using their tickets and corporate travel cards.

• The business unit relies on client retention and the long term customer relationships which, of late, had been under considerable pressure due to competitor offerings and the stigma of fraud and misuse associated with their Cabcharge products.

 

Together we identified and aligned on expectations and were able to construct a shared overall vision of what the travel management system would encompass.

 

We then took this vision into the research phase to confirm or disapprove our hypothesis of why a travel management system was needed.

Before jumping into a solution driven driven by the business and competitor research findings, we set out to perform a set of contextual inquiries and interviews with Cabcharge's top 10 clients (Which became our test pilot users that we would continuously engage with to get feedback and refine our designs). We engaged them with a list of questions and system activities in order to understand their pain points and better define their needs.

 

Key findings:

• As Cabcharge’s CTMS system was very out of date in terms of functionality, look and feel, users found it difficult to use and not as user friendly or intuitive as its competitors tools.

• We discovered that the administration burden associated with cabcharge products was a more frequent recurring pain point than that of fraud and misuse.

Looking at the research from our contextual inquiries I created an overall customer journey map to visualise and communicate the users end‐to‐end experience across various touch‐points of Cabcharge products.

 

These maps indicated to us that we were to focus our attention on the three most common pain points discovered;

• Administration

• Fraud

• Misuse

I then conducted a competitive analysis to contrast the key features, offerings, and types of digital platforms available amongst their competitors.

 

Key insight:

although Cabcharge held a niche with its Fastcard and e-ticket products, its product management offering (CTMS) was very limited in comparison to its competitors as they were offering real time trip information, trip visibility and user accountability to help bypass travel fraud and misuse.

 

Through careful analysis of our research, we identified sufficient behavioural variables to segment our user audience and were able to create a set of 5 personas.

 

We used these personas consistently throughout the project to guide design decisions, feature priorities, and to create empathy amongst the stakeholders and our team.

 

Armed with all our research I entered all the features into a spreadsheet and prioritised them against our 3 focus areas, personas needs, tech feasibility, and business objectives.

the solution.

Cabcharge approached us with two primary objectives:

 

1) To create a travel management system in response to their clients needs for better administration and control over their Cabcharge products. Cabcharge also wanted this system to enhance their products functionality and usability beyond their competitors.

 

2) To introduce and implement an agile approach to product design, delivery and collaboration within the company.

A complementary online application for Cabcharge clients to help support their Cabcharge travel. The key focus was to create better visibility of employee travel and spend to help solve and combat the increasing occurrence of travel fraud and misuse.

Create a high performing agile delivery team to support the Corporate Accounts sector within the business.

usability testing

In addition to delivering the UX experience for a responsive site, I was engaged to create new logo, look and feel for Cabcharge plus. After conducting a brand analysis I created a look that was modern, fresh, pain-free, softer, simple and professional.

As we were working in an agile 2 week sprint format, we started by off developing the highest prioritised features off the backlog to create an MVP for launch.

 

I would use the following design development cycle to ensure selected features were successfully deployed at the end of the sprint. This process also gave us the confidence that when launched, the travel management system would work as intended, and be simple and fun to use.

I would start by envisioning a number of user stories and flows which would help drive and validate the designs.

Next steps would generally involve facilitating a meeting with the team to see what design is most desirable in terms of technical difficultly and sprint time restraints or host a design thinking workshop to evolve a design or vision further within the team.

having a clearer vision of the design I would then build an interactive prototype for usability testing.  I would use Axure for prototypes that required medium to high interactivity and  Photoshop/Illustrator + Invision for those that required more high fidelity designs.

Throughout the development stage I would help monitor and manage any design development changes.

Testing the prototype would be the next key step in allowing us to confidently iterate and refine on our designs.

Test cycle 1) Concept testing

Test cycle 2) Design tweaking

Test cycle 3) Finalising design

If visual design was required I would implement it after the prototype was finalised to support rapid prototyping techniques.
