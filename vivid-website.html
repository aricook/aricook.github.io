the brief.

Vivid Sydney is an 18 day festival of light music and ideas and for the second year running Canon is a proud sponsor.

The brief was to design a platform to display educational, informational social content designed to engage consumers in Canon pre, during and post Vivid.

 

I researched competitive sites to define the market scope, familiarise myself with competitor sites and find what on-boarding processes and community-based features are typically used to engage consumers.

This project had such a broad target audience that it needed to work for many different personas. I took that knowledge and refined my research findings to deliver an encouraging, inspiring and simple responsive website.

Being supplied with the site content I began structuring a simple sitemap.

I then used the sitemap to conceptualise and then define all possible user journeys to visualise all the relevant functionality of the site.

vivid microsite

An adaptive, pre, during and post event website.

PART 1

Having identified 6 key screens through the sitemap, I created wireframes. I worked on reformatting the content of each screen from the content given to me and simplified the information and interactions between each of the pages.

Developers were then introduced to the project and I worked with them to thoroughly scrutinise the website, preparing it for development. I went through a series of iterations on the wireframes, taking into account new considerations that arose as we fine tuned the design.

 

The wireframes were then brought to life with inspiring images and a striking interface design.
