personas

 

We created 7 personas to humanise the relationship between the brand and its target audience. Given the broad target audience it was necessary to segment them into groups, from which we could ensure the website had the functionality to cater for everyone.

 

workshops

 

I hosted a workshop with current WSU students to find out how they felt about the open day experience, what their pain points and highlights were. From this we found how important it was for future students to get past students validation.

We then conducted a feature prioritisation exercise of the current site with the students. From this we found how important the campus life and activities were to students, and that 90% were not aware of the social events and activities available at WSU.

 

During the workshop discussions were guided by the “5 W’s and an H” structure.

We discovered the following information throughout this research phase:

• The audiences demographics and psychographics

• What people used and intended to use within the Open Day site

• Where the users might perform certain tasks

What open day features intrigued them most why, such as physical locations and programs

How the users performed certain tasks, which provided us with certain details on what explicit actions and steps the user took to reach their goal.

 

 

Stakeholder workshops and Feature prioritisation

 

With my competitive analysis, user workshops and user research in mind I hosted a design thinking workshop with the stakeholders to establish potential features for the open day site for pre, during and post event.

We ended up with a collection of features which we placed on a feature prioritisation map on levels of high to low technical difficulty and nice-to-have to essential.

 

User Journeys

 

I put together user journeys for each persona to establish and show all the relevant functionality within the site. These user journeys helped determine and prioritise the feature set for the website and to started to inform the Information Architecture.

Sitemap

 

I created a sitemap which included a breakdown of the sections, categories, purpose, functionality and content for the different sections of the WSU open day website.

With the user journeys, site map and personas approved by all stakeholders I started wireframing in Axure to show relevant interactions and overall site flow structure.

This wireframing stage was managed using an iterative/sprint based approach. I interacted with both the project manager and developers on a regular basis to ensure that the designs were achievable within both the time and budget scopes.

The wireframes were then tested and iterated, leaving us with a motivating yet sophisticated contemporary interface design.

I started off by researching the competitive landscape to see what other universities were hosting at their open day to attract their potential future students, Pre, During and after the event.  I discovered best and worse practices and started to work out where WSU’s offering could sit within the market.
