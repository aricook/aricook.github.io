Key research insight:

 

Once a Giga Selfie is taken a video file is created and could be shared via email and posted to any social media channels.

I did some research amongst the types of people that would be attending the event in order to best understand my target audience and inform my design decisions throughout the project.

As this event would include 50% Chinese and 50% Australian users this heavily influenced technical requirements (what apps would be used for video download) and overall user journey to accommodate for Chinese speaking tourists. From this discovery we decided that we would enable sharing via WeChat, email and Instagram.

the brief

the solution.

As part of Sydney Chinese New year celebrations, Tourism Australia partnered with Canon to give  visitors the chance to mark the celebration by getting the best possible selfie souvenir of their time in Australia - a Giga Selfie overlooking the world-famous Sydney Harbour Bridge and Opera House.

A Giga Selfie is a short video clip which starts as a close up selfie and zooms out to reveal your spectacular surroundings.

I was briefed to create the best possible experience for the event itself, a microsite to promote the event and download your Giga Selfie, and an iPad web app for staff to trigger the selfie.

I designed a streamlined and engaging user experience encompassing multiple touch points and devices- Which made news headlines!

Iterative design

I constructed a user journey map to show a step by step process of the experience to internal and external stake holders.

Developers were then brought into the project and I worked with them closely to thoroughly scrutinise the process.. After a series of iterations I fine tuned the experience to take on any additional technical requirements that came up along the way- like how we could trigger the camera to take a photo without running a 1km cable across the harbour bridge!
